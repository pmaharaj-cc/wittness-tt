# Problem Statement

## Core problem

Public trust in Trinidad & Tobago **cars for hire** has collapsed because serious harm occurred on **both sides** of the passenger–driver relationship — and existing workarounds do not prevent it in real time.

## Who is harmed

- **Children and parents** — especially households without private cars, relying on hire vehicles for school, family visits, lessons, and rural travel.
- **Drivers** — including good-faith operators coerced into dangerous detours or robbed by passengers.
- **The wider public** — loss of a transport option that many communities depend on.

## What success looks like (concept-level)

1. A parent can send a child on a hire trip with **verifiable trip integrity** without the child carrying a smartphone.
2. A driver can **refuse or flag unsafe requests** with community or platform backing.
3. Online volunteers contribute **structured witness** — not Facebook pile-ons — via sanitized data.
4. **Rural trips** receive proportionally **more attention**, with honest messaging about response limits.
5. Escalation exists for **stale trips** and **panic** without volunteers pretending to be emergency services.

## Success metrics (when built)

| Metric | Target |
|--------|--------|
| Median time-to-initiate trip | < 30 seconds |
| Median time-to-close after arrival | < 15 seconds |
| Rural trips reaching confirm quorum within 15 min of delivery | Track vs urban baseline |
| False panic / abuse rate | < 5% of escalations |

## Non-goals (concept phase)

- Replacing 999 or TTPS
- Mandating driver GPS tracking
- Building a full Uber competitor before trust is restored
- Collecting durable PII on children

## MVP wedge

The Week-1 prototype ([`../mvp/README.md`](../mvp/README.md)) collapses the vision to one gamified task: **remote guardians confirm delivery** on a scroll feed — while parents hold the authoritative start/end confirm and panic button.
