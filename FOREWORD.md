# Foreword

*A personal note from the person who put this together.*

---

In Trinidad and Tobago it is very easy to **talk**.

We know the type,
plenty big talk on Facebook, in Parliament yards, in comment sections, at the barber, on the radio call-in. Profile picture with a flag filter. Long post about justice. Tag twelve people. Disappear when something actually needs doing for months on end. Or worse: show up only when there is credit to take, a contract to angle for, or outrage to farm before the next news cycle.

I am not against passion. I am against **performance without follow-through** especially when children and hire-car trust are involved and parents are left photographing licence plates because nobody built anything they can actually use.

**WiTTness** is not that.

I write this, not as a visitor to the conversation, but as family on both sides of the water. The hire-car fear hits home whether you are in Moruga or Mississauga. A parent in Trinidad should not need a cousin in Toronto to explain a safety idea in a voice note; they should need a **link**. And when the spec says a WiTTness in POS can confirm a trip in Cedros, it also means a Trini abroad, scrolling at 3 p.m. their time, can be part of the same national witness pool, because **protect our kids** does not stop at immigration. We already have the diaspora, the phones, and the will. Use it.

This is not a launch party. There is no personality cult here. No one needs to explain it to you in a voice note or beg a minister to cut a ribbon. The README is public. The demo runs in your browser. The specification is written. The license is MIT. Fork it, host it, improve it, ignore it, but nobody has to perform for you to see that **someone did something**.

Here is what I believe:

**We already have what we need to take care of each other.**

Not in the abstract. In the concrete.

- Parents who will tap twice if it means their child is witnessed home.
- Aunties and uncles and retirees and underemployed people already online for hours, not wasted, but **redirected** into one confirm button on a sanitized trip card.
- Community instinct, even the *macco* energy people joke about, which, structured and moderated, is **eyes on the road** instead of pile-ons after harm.
- Phones, SMS, zones instead of addresses, rush-hour buffers so we do not cry wolf every traffic jam.
- Open source so the idea belongs to no one politician and no one company.

We do not need another Trini saviour on a stage. We do not need a foundation with a logo before a child is safe in a hire car. We need **use**: of time, of attention, of the fact that a trip can be registered in thirty seconds and witnessed by strangers who care because the feed is right there between the TikToks.

**Protect our kids** is not a slogan for a T-shirt. It is an instruction: use what we already have.

If this repo is still here in a year and nothing shipped, then it failed like the big-talk posts it was written against. If one parent tries the demo, or one developer forks it, or one WiTTness confirms a trip on a real build, then the talk was worth less than the tap.

No hero or funds required. Just use it.
